Broadway & Show Tunes

The drama, the lights, the emotion

Throughout my life, I was introduced to various Broadway productions. My mom took me to see Wicked when I was very young, along with a few smaller musicals, but at the time I was too little for the music or storytelling to truly resonate. I dabbled in different cast recordings here and there, but I always preferred when a musical had a film adaptation I could watch—like Les Misérables. I remember when the Hamilton soundtrack came out and many of my friends would sing it during recess. I gave it a listen, but I honestly didn’t get the hype and just felt confused.

That all changed around the start of COVID-19. With more time at home and Hamilton released on Disney+, I gave it another shot—and this time, something clicked. I ended up watching it multiple times that month. From there, I went down a rabbit hole on YouTube, discovering clips from other Broadway productions and slowly adding their soundtracks to my Spotify playlists. That’s when I began to truly appreciate the complexity of the music, the vocal talent required, and the theatrical depth that show tunes offer.

Some of my favorite musicals include Hamilton, Dear Evan Hansen, Mean Girls, and Heathers: The Musical, but there are three shows that stand out as my absolute favorites. First is Beetlejuice, a hilarious and high-energy adaptation of the 1988 film. Second is Hadestown, a beautiful and haunting retelling of the Greek tragedy of Orpheus and Eurydice. And third is SIX: The Musical, a modern reimagining of the six wives of King Henry VIII told through powerhouse pop performances. There's something truly awe-inspiring about the raw vocals, layered harmonies, and rich storytelling these shows deliver.

Show tunes taught me that music can be dramatic, emotional, and narratively powerful. Every character has a story, and every note is intentional. These musicals helped shape my love of storytelling and inspired me to see music as a way to express complex emotions and ideas. I've been lucky enough to see a few productions both on tour and on Broadway—and I hope to experience many more in the future.
